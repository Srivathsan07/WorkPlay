Flex to work and play

Connecting AFLW players with flexible employment opportunities.

Flexible work is essential to allow AFLW athletes to thrive on and off the field. Employment throughout their playing careers sets players up for a successful transition when they reach the end of their playing careers.

Why employ an AFLW player?

AFLW players are highly motivated and driven people. Many of the skills they possess translate into being a great employee. AFLW players excel under pressure, they know how to deliver and time manage, they are high performing team members and they are incredible leaders on and off the field.

Many of these attributes are hard to acquire, success in the sporting arena requires the ability to take on feedback and hone skills for better performance, making AFLW players wonderful potential employees.

Flexible work requirements

AFLW player train and play up to 20 hours a week during pre-season and the home and away season. To balance a non-playing career, flexibility in start and end times is essential to support them to attend mid-week training and games. During the off season, they may wish to increase hours. Each athlete will have their own skillset and flexibility requirements.

Testimonials

Jana Lanigan

People Manager, John Holland Gold Coast Light Rail Project

Tara Bohanna works in our Safety team on the Gold Coast Light Rail (GCLR) project based in Broadbeach as a Safety Coordinator. I assisted in Tara’s on-boarding to John Holland when she joined the Queensland Urban Utilities project last year, and again when joining the GCLR team. Being an avid AFLW supporter and understanding the AFLW’s journey to date, I recognised what was needed to make a role workable for Tara. I was able to explain and champion her cause to our Project Director so that we could find her a place on the team that matched her experience and to work in with the timing that she needed. We found that happy balance and it is all going well. Tara has been a great addition to the team and we all love watching her journey with the Gold Coast SUNS.

Tara Bohana

John Holland Group Safety Coordinator,Gold Coast Suns AFLW

Female athletes should be empowered to be their absolute best on and off the field – and that means having access to great career opportunities. Working with John Holland has helped me develop skills and experience in construction and set up an exciting future on projects both big and small. Most importantly, I’ve still got the flexibility I need to train and play my best footy.

Renee Tierney

Essendon AFLW - Trainee, AFL Communications and Corporate Affairs

Having the opportunity to work at the AFL has been extremely rewarding for me. I have been able to explore the business side of the sport I am passionate about. On my traineeship basis I have been involved in some really cool projects. This includes being in discussions and events, that are later tangible in the media. It has been equally interesting to see the way the business is conducted - sharing both an employee, player and fan perspective. My communications and corporate affairs team also offers me a wealth of support off the field and away from the desk. They have complete understanding of my situation and it allows flexibility for me and my work hours. It's great to be able to work with others who share a genuine interest and care for me and footy.’

Nina Morrison

Geelong AFLW, AFL Data Analytics team

Nina has been an incredible addition to the AFL Data & Analytics team. Her passion for data with the added bonus of her knowledge of football has enabled her to have an immediate impact. Despite working with many complicated datasets and new technologies, I have been impressed by Nina’s ability to run with new projects. The qualities required for a professional athlete certainly transfer over to Nina’s role on the Data team – she is always a team player, maintains a positive mindset and she has a strong commitment to excellence. I would highly recommend any future employer to consider AFLW players for their business.

Nina Morrison

Geelong AFLW, AFL Data Analytics team

Working part time within the AFL's Data and Analytics team has been an extremely rewarding and enjoyable experience. As a workplace, the AFL has built a strong culture centered around its genuine care for its people. The flexibility afforded to my work schedule makes juggling the commitments of AFLW much more manageable, as I'm supported and encouraged to work from where it suits best, have adjustable finishing hours, and shuffle my days if required. As an employer, the AFL has a unique understanding of the pressures of AFLW and the intensity of my training requirements, so there is no added stress of needing to legitimize or justify the hours and energy which I need to direct towards my football. It is awesome to have so many people within the organization engaged and invested in how the team and I are going.
